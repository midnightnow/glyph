# Uppercase Geometric Style Guide: The Torque System

This guide defines the aesthetic and structural requirements for a typeface designed to inhabit the Model 2 (13-node) Compass Rose. In this system, glyphs are not static shapes but **directional vectors** resulting from a rotational "spin" (CCW vs CW) at 13 harmonic stations.

## 1. Core Aesthetic Principles

### 1.1. Ductus as Pathfinding
The "beauty" of a letter is measured by its **tangential plausibility**. If the pen starts at a node, does the stroke follow the compass energy of that node?
- **Avoid**: Static "L-shapes" or "I-beams" that kill momentum.
- **Embrace**: Arcs, hooks, and diagonal thrusts that suggest the letter is a snapshot of a moving object.

### 1.2. The SW Sinusoidal Standard
The font's weight and slant should be calibrated to the **S (CCW) @ 249.23°**. This is the "Primary Torque" position.
- The principal axis of stress (the thinnest/thickest parts of an 'O') should align with this bearing.
- The "slant" of the typeface should be influenced by the pull of the Southwest quadrant.

### 1.3. Terminal Directionality
Serifs and terminals are not decorative; they are **vectors**.
- **CCW Terminals**: Should flare upwards or outwards, suggesting centrifugal escape.
- **CW Terminals**: Should hook inwards or downwards, suggesting centripetal weight.

## 2. Structural Requirements by Strength

### Type A: The Rotational Core (✅ Strong)
*C, G, J, Q, S, U*
- These letters should be the "heroes" of the font. 
- Maximize curvature.
- The 'G' hook and 'Q' tail should be treated as precise exits from their respective bearings (E and SSW).

### Type B: The Transitional Lattice (⚠️ Partial)
*K, M, N, O, P, V, W, X, Y, Z*
- These letters must be "tilted" or "sheared" to avoid static symmetry.
- 'O' must have a diagonal stress axis.
- 'M' and 'N' should emphasize diagonal tension over vertical stability.

### Type C: The Anchor/Friction Points (❌ Weak/Static)
*A, B, D, E, F, H, I, L, T*
- These letters resist rotation. 
- **Design Strategy**: Instead of fighting the static nature, treat them as the "braking system" of the compass. Use sharp serifs to pin them to their bearings, or introduce calligraphic "swash" elements to force a directional bias.

---

## 3. Recommended Font DNA

To meet these requirements, search for or design fonts with the following "DNA":
- **Humanist Calligraphy**: e.g., *Chancery Italic*, *Sabon*, *Garamond Premr Pro*.
- **High-Contrast Serifs**: e.g., *Bodoni* (if italics are handled dynamically).
- **Expansionist Scripts**: e.g., *Copperplate* (where the pressure of the nib creates the weight).
