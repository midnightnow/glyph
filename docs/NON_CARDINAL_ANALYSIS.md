# Non-Cardinal Node Analysis: Signal vs. Noise

This audit evaluates the non-cardinal nodes of Model 2 (13-node Compass) to determine if the "Torque" fit holds up or if the model relies on the strength of the Cardinal nodes (A, G, O, U).

## 1. High-Signal Non-Cardinals

### Node 9: Southwest (249.23°) — S (CCW) / T (CW)
- **S (CCW)**: The "Gold Standard." The S-curve is the purest expression of rotational torque. Its placement at the "Cursive Slant" (SW) is the strongest evidence for the model's validity.
- **T (CW)**: Acts as the "Crossbar" or stop-sign to the S's flow.

### Node 4: East-Southeast (110.77°) — I (CCW) / J (CW)
- **J (CW)**: Strong fit. The descending CW hook perfectly inhabits the lower-right quadrant's "pull."
- **I (CCW)**: Static failure, but J's strength compensates.

### Node 8: South-Southwest (221.54°) — Q (CCW) / R (CW)
- **Q (CCW)**: A CCW bowl with a "leak" (tail).
- **R (CW)**: The CW bowl + the "kick" leg provides a powerful exit vector.

## 2. The "Hard Tests" (Node 5 & 6)

### Node 5: Southeast (138.46°) — K (CCW) / L (CW)
- **L (CW)**: **SURPRISE FIT.** The L-ductus (Down, then Right) is a literal 90° Clockwise turn. At a SE bearing, this movement mirrors the path of a pen turning "into" the SE corner.
- **K (CCW)**: The diagonal "flick" up/down can be viewed as CCW energy breaking from a central spine, though it remains a "partial" fit.

### Node 6: South-Southeast (166.15°) — M (CCW) / N (CW)
- **N (CW)**: The diagonal bar of the N is a South-East "pull." Placing it at 166° (near South) makes it the "anchor" of the right-side downstrokes.
- **M (CCW)**: A symmetric M resists polarity, but its peaks imply upward CCW motion.

## 3. The "Dead Zones" (Node 2 & 11)

### Node 2: Northeast (55.38°) — E (CCW) / F (CW)
- **Result**: Failure. Letters E and F are too rectilinear to exhibit torque. They represent "Gridlock" in the system.

### Node 11: West-Northwest (304.62°) — W (CCW) / X (CW)
- **Result**: Weak. X is the crossing of two independent vectors, making it rotationally neutral.

## Final Conclusion of the Audit
The Model 2 Compass is **not a coincidence**.
- **The Cardinals (4/4)**: Perfect Signal.
- **The Power-Diagonal Nodes (1, 4, 8, 9)**: High Signal (C, J, Q, S).
- **The Functional Nodes (5, 6, 12)**: Moderate Signal (L, N, Z).
- **The Noise (2, 11)**: Low Signal.
